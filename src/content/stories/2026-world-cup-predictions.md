The 2026 World Cup begins on June 11. Three host nations — the United States, Canada, Mexico — 48 teams for the first time in history, and the first tournament where Argentina enter as defending champions with Lionel Messi, at 38 years old, potentially playing his final game of football on the biggest stage there is.

I have watched every World Cup since 1998. I have been a Brazil supporter since the night I watched them lose to France, in a neighbourhood that went suddenly, impossibly quiet. I have never not picked Brazil. I have never not been disappointed. I am picking Brazil again.

This is not irrational. It is something more complicated than irrational.

---

## What Makes 2026 Different From Every Previous Tournament?

48 teams is a meaningful expansion from 32. [FIFA's rationale](https://www.fifa.com/fifaplus/en/articles/fifa-world-cup-2026-format-explained) is partly commercial — more nations, more markets — but the on-pitch effect is real: the group stage now uses three-team groups with a play-in round for runners-up. More games. More upsets. More noise before the signal.

The favourites remain: Brazil, Argentina, France, England, Germany. But the shape of the competition has changed. A 48-team tournament rewards depth and squad rotation in ways a 32-team tournament did not. Nations with weaker first elevens but stronger squads will go further. Nations that rely heavily on individual brilliance — which is to say, nations whose tournament lives or dies with one man — face a different kind of pressure.

This matters for Argentina. It matters for anyone who assumes the 2022 result simply repeats.

---

## Why Am I Still Picking Brazil?

Brazil have not won a World Cup since 2002. That is twenty-four years of near-misses, exits that felt inevitable only in retrospect, and a country that treats football as something closer to national theology than sport.

The 2026 squad is built around [Vinicius Jr](https://www.transfermarkt.com/vinicius-junior/profil/spieler/371998), who at 25 is arguably the most complete attacker in world football. He is fast in a way that creates problems before he touches the ball. He is two-footed. He makes decisions at speed that other attackers make standing still.

But Vinicius Jr is not the only reason I am picking Brazil. This squad, unlike 2014 or 2018, has no obvious structural weakness. The goalkeeper is capable. The defence is solid. The midfield, which has been Brazil's problem for a decade, has more depth than any Brazil team since the Ronaldinho era.

I am also picking Brazil because I always pick Brazil. That is not logic. That is identity. The two are not always distinguishable.

---

## What Is the Case Against Brazil?

Brazil have not won a World Cup since 2002. That sentence works in both directions.

The pressure on a Brazilian team at a World Cup is not comparable to the pressure on any other team. [The country stops](https://apnews.com/article/world-cup-brazil-fans-watching-soccer). The expectations are metaphysical. They are about what Brazil means to itself and to the people who have loved this team across generations.

That pressure produces strange football. Conservatism when Brazil should attack. Individual moments of genius that do not connect to collective coherence. A 7-1 loss at home. Several exits that should not have happened.

I am aware that I pick Brazil every four years and Brazil breaks my heart every four years. I am picking them anyway.

---

## Which Team Will Actually Win the 2026 World Cup?

France.

I say this with the resignation of someone who finds France difficult to love but impossible to dismiss. [Kylian Mbappé](https://www.transfermarkt.com/kylian-mbappe/profil/spieler/342229) at 27 will be at the exact peak of what he is capable of. The French squad combines individual quality with tactical discipline in a way no other team matches. They have depth at every position. They do not rely on one player the way Argentina relies on Messi.

They are probably the best team in the world heading into this tournament. They are the team I least want to win.

---

## Will Messi Play — And Does It Matter?

This is the conversation everyone is having and nobody wants to answer directly: will Messi play, and if he does, can Argentina go back-to-back?

Messi will be 38 during the tournament. [He is still playing in MLS](https://www.mlssoccer.com/players/lionel-messi), still at a level that makes people stop. But playing at a level that makes people stop in MLS and carrying Argentina through a 48-team World Cup are different burdens on the same body.

My honest assessment: Argentina can reach the final. Getting through the first three rounds behind a 38-year-old Messi, across multiple games in three weeks, is the challenge. If he stays fit and close to his 2022 level, Argentina are a serious contender. If he does not — if the schedule does what schedules do to 38-year-old bodies — Argentina are a team of good players without a transcendent one.

I hope he plays. I hope he plays every minute. Even as a Brazil fan, I hope this.

---

## My Final Predictions

**Winner:** France  
**Runner-up:** Brazil  
**Semi-finalists:** Argentina, England  
**Dark horse:** Morocco — they were not a quarter-final fluke in 2022  
**Exit that will surprise everyone:** Germany, earlier than expected

And Brazil will play beautifully. And something will happen — a refereeing decision, an injury, a penalty shootout — and I will watch in silence, the way crowds have always gone quiet when Brazil loses, and then I will talk about the next four years.

That is what it is to love a team. You already know how it ends. You watch anyway.
